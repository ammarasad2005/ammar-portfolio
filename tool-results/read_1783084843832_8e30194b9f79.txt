    81→    81→### Round 1 Synthesis (Batches 1-4, 20 portfolios) — Codified signals
    82→    82→
    83→    83→After 20 portfolios, six signals are stable enough to call "codified":
    84→    84→
    85→    85→1. **Commitment beats kitchen-sink.** Cade (1 keyframe) and Whilmar (3 keyframes + WebGL) both feel senior because each commits to a register. Ghulam (86 keyframes) does not. The whole site must sing in one key.
    86→    86→2. **Tinted-neutral background, never pure.** Warm-tinted lights (peach `rgb(254,215,170)`, cream) and tinted-darks (navy `#1E2749`, near-black-warm `#0D0D0E`) both work. Pure black/white is what to avoid — *unless* paired with ultralight (200-300 weight) display type at very large sizes (Electric Magic Factory's 109px weight-200 Soehne on pure white). The pure-white escape hatch requires typographic confidence.
    87→    87→3. **Domain-native conceptual metaphor shapes content architecture.** Triet (Unix shell), Whilmar (architectural-engineering), Zyon (Steam achievements) — each reframes section labels, hero metadata, and footer copy through the dev's domain. The metaphor shapes content structure, not just decoration.
    88→    88→4. **Two-font developer-coded feel.** Workhorse sans (Inter / Geist Sans / Soehne) + monospace eyebrows on labels (`// about`, `// work`). Universal, low-risk, high-signal.
    89→    89→5. **Hero-only home + linked content subpages.** Nico Bachner (`/projects` + `/notes`), Ajvad (Astro view transitions), EMF (Vue view transitions). The home page is a brand statement; depth lives one click away. Multi-page hybrid with view transitions is the leading architecture.
    90→    90→6. **Footer is a real design surface.** Whilmar's `V2.0.26_STABLE` version stamp, EMF's live local time + timezone + location, Ajvad's live date stamp — the footer carries "site is alive" signals that undermine the static-portfolio feeling.
    91→    91→
    92→    92→**Newly codified (round 1)**:
    93→    93→
    94→    94→7. **Typography Options Panel as code-as-interface.** EMF's live type-scale editor (12 named ratios from Minor Second 1.067 through Octave 2 + Custom) is the first non-cosmetic "code-as-interface" pattern. It positions the developer's *craft* (typography engineering) as the central interactive exhibit. Partial resolution of the "code-as-interface" tension.
    95→    95→8. **View Transitions API with shared-element names.** EMF uses `view-transition-name: main` + `title-1` + `title-2` for designed blur+opacity page transitions. This is the first *designed* page-transition pattern (not a browser default).
    96→    96→9. **Single accent applied only to the role phrase.** Phat Tran Tan applies the accent color *only* to the role phrase inside the H1. Concentrates attention without colorizing the entire hero.
    97→    97→10. **Pull-over keyframe.** Barthélémy's sticky-note tab slides up to become the page — an original page-entry transition. Steal the principle (an element from the previous screen "becomes" the next), not the literal sticky-note.

   167→   167→## 2. Pattern Library (Cumulative)
   168→   168→
   169→   169→### Hero-as-Brand-Statement
   170→   170→- **Sources**: Jeremiah Haastrup, Nico Bachner
   171→   171→- **Why it works**: A single saturated color or warm tint + bold typographic statement reads as confident. Removes the pressure to fill a long page.
   172→   172→- **Possible improvements**: Pair with one secondary "deep dive" surface so the confidence doesn't read as evasiveness. Nico's `/projects` + `/notes` solves this.
   173→   173→- **Compatibility**: Pairs with two-font system, hero-only home + content subpages.
   174→   174→- **Complexity**: low
   175→   175→- **Performance**: excellent
   176→   176→- **Accessibility**: requires care with color contrast on saturated backgrounds.
   177→   177→- **Adopt**: yes — as hero philosophy, with linked content subpages for depth.
   178→   178→
   179→   179→### Domain-Native Conceptual Metaphor
   180→   180→- **Sources**: Triet Thai (Unix shell), Whilmar Bitoco (architectural-engineering), Zyon Tiangson (Steam achievements)
   181→   181→- **Why it works**: Reframes section labels, hero metadata, and footer copy through the dev's domain. Signals "I think in this medium" instantly.
   182→   182→- **Possible improvements**: For full-stack, choose a metaphor broad enough to span frontend/backend/infra/design — without becoming a diluted "tech-y" aesthetic. The metaphor should shape *labels* (eyebrows, metadata, version stamps), not force awkward content shapes.
   183→   183→- **Compatibility**: Strong with mono-eyebrow labels. Conflicts with Brutalism or playful illustration.
   184→   184→- **Complexity**: medium (requires content discipline)
   185→   185→- **Performance**: excellent
   186→   186→- **Accessibility**: AT reads metaphor syntax literally — need `aria-label` overrides.
   187→   187→- **Adopt**: yes — as a *light* metaphor (monospace metadata eyebrows, version stamps, status strings).
   188→   188→
   189→   189→### Two-Font Developer-Coded Feel
   190→   190→- **Sources**: Antônio Junior, Triet Thai (single-font extreme), Whilmar Bitoco, Nico Bachner, EMF
   191→   191→- **Why it works**: Workhorse sans (Inter/Geist/Soehne) + monospace eyebrows on labels (`// about`, `// work`) reads as "developer" without green-on-black terminal cliché.
   192→   192→- **Possible improvements**: Vary the eyebrow treatment; don't apply the same `// X` formula to every section.
   193→   193→- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   194→   194→- **Complexity**: low
   195→   195→- **Performance**: trivial
   196→   196→- **Accessibility**: Excellent — mono labels are short and screen-reader friendly.
   197→   197→- **Adopt**: yes — primary candidate for our design system.
   198→   198→
   199→   199→### Metadata-as-Hero-Content
   200→   200→- **Sources**: Triet Thai (`loc:`, `uptime:`, `status:`), Whilmar (`REF:` / `ARCHITECTURAL SCALE:`), Zyon (Steam-style metadata)
   201→   201→- **Why it works**: Replaces the cliché "Hi, I'm X. A passionate Y" with terse machine-readable metadata. Reads as confident, current, and developer-native.
   202→   202→- **Possible improvements**: Limit to 3-4 fields (location, status, focus, availability). Use `<dl>` for clean AT output.
   203→   203→- **Compatibility**: Pairs with mono-eyebrow pattern and domain-native metaphor.
   204→   204→- **Complexity**: low
   205→   205→- **Performance**: trivial
   206→   206→- **Accessibility**: Use `<dl><dt><dd>` structure.
   207→   207→- **Adopt**: yes.
   208→   208→
   209→   209→### Hero-Only Home + Content Subpages
   210→   210→- **Sources**: Nico Bachner (`/projects` + `/notes`), Ajvad Laseen (Astro view transitions), EMF (Vue view transitions)
   211→   211→- **Why it works**: Home page is a brand statement; depth lives one click away. Combines confidence of hero-only with depth of multi-page.
   212→   212→- **Possible improvements**: Use View Transitions API with shared-element names for designed page transitions (EMF pattern).
   213→   213→- **Compatibility**: Pairs with hero-as-brand-statement, view transitions.
   214→   214→- **Complexity**: medium
   215→   215→- **Performance**: excellent (static-rendered subpages)
   216→   216→- **Accessibility**: Standard nav semantics.
   217→   217→- **Adopt**: yes — primary architecture. *(Note: project constraint — only `/` route is user-visible. Will adapt to single-page with section "routes" via scroll/anchor + modal case studies.)*
   218→   218→
   219→   219→### Tinted-Neutral Background
   220→   220→- **Sources**: Nico (peach `rgb(254,215,170)`), Whilmar (near-black-warm `#0D0D0E`), Ajvad (navy `#1E2749`), EMF (pure white with ultralight type)
   221→   221→- **Why it works**: Tinted neutrals feel intentional; pure black/white feel default.
   222→   222→- **Possible improvements**: Provide both light + dark themes; tint both (warm cream light, warm-black dark). Pure white only with ultralight display type.
   223→   223→- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   224→   224→- **Complexity**: low
   225→   225→- **Performance**: trivial
   226→   226→- **Accessibility**: Must meet contrast AA in both themes.
   227→   227→- **Adopt**: yes — tinted light + tinted dark; user-toggle with system preference default.
   228→   228→
   229→   229→### Numbered Projects
   230→   230→- **Sources**: Phat Tran Tan (`01 02 03 04`)
   231→   231→- **Why it works**: Indexes the work without showing it all at once. Reads as catalog, not gallery.
   232→   232→- **Possible improvements**: Use as nav for a project list view, not just decorative.
   233→   233→- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   234→   234→- **Complexity**: low
   235→   235→- **Performance**: trivial
   236→   236→- **Accessibility**: Provide `aria-label="Project 1: Name"`.
   237→   237→- **Adopt**: yes.
   238→   238→
   239→   239→### Frosted-Glass Sticky Header
   240→   240→- **Sources**: Phat Tran Tan (`backdrop-blur`)
   241→   241→- **Why it works**: Header stays accessible without occupying solid space; the blur signals "layer above content."
   242→   242→- **Possible improvements**: Pair with a subtle hairline border that fades in on scroll.
   243→   243→- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   244→   244→- **Complexity**: low
   245→   245→- **Performance**: backdrop-filter has cost on low-end mobile — provide fallback.
   246→   246→- **Accessibility**: Ensure contrast remains AA when content scrolls behind.
   247→   247→- **Adopt**: yes.
   248→   248→
   249→   249→### Single Accent on Role Phrase
   250→   250→- **Sources**: Phat Tran Tan
   251→   251→- **Why it works**: Concentrates attention without colorizing the entire hero.
   252→   252→- **Possible improvements**: Use the accent consistently across the site for *only* the most important action (CTA, current section indicator, active link).
   253→   253→- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   254→   254→- **Complexity**: low
   255→   255→- **Performance**: trivial
   256→   256→- **Accessibility**: Don't rely on color alone.
   257→   257→- **Adopt**: yes.
   258→   258→
   259→   259→### Live Date/Time Stamp in Footer
   260→   260→- **Sources**: Ajvad Laseen, EMF (with timezone + location)
   261→   261→- **Why it works**: Cheapest "site is alive" signal. Undermines the static-portfolio feeling.
   262→   262→- **Possible improvements**: Include timezone. Update via `requestAnimationFrame`-throttled `setInterval`. Avoid SSR/CSR mismatch by hydrating after mount.
   263→   263→- **Compatibility**: Pairs with footer-as-design-surface.
   264→   264→- **Complexity**: low
   265→   265→- **Performance**: trivial

   379→   379→## 5. Provisional Design Principles (Revised Continuously)
   380→   380→
   381→   381→1. **Restraint over abundance.** Premium feels come from what is *removed*, not what is added.
   382→   382→2. **Motion must mean something.** No decorative animations; every transition should orient, reveal, or reward.
   383→   383→3. **Typography is the interface.** Most "premium" portfolios lean on type, not chrome.
   384→   384→4. **One signature conceptual metaphor** — not just one signature interaction. The metaphor shapes content structure, not just decoration.
   385→   385→5. **Performance is a feature.** A portfolio that loads fast *feels* premium before any visual registers.
   386→   386→6. **Curate ruthlessly.** Three excellent projects beat thirty decent ones.
   387→   387→7. **The tab title is part of the design.** Never leave it as the framework default.
   388→   388→8. **Commit to a register.** Both minimalism and richness work; what fails is the kitchen-sink middle. Pick a register and commit. *(Added after Batch 3.)*
   389→   389→9. **Tinted neutrals, never pure.** Unless paired with ultralight display type at very large sizes. *(Added after Batch 2, refined after Batch 4.)*
   390→   390→10. **The footer is a design surface, not boilerplate.** Use it for "site is alive" signals. *(Added after Batch 3.)*
   391→   391→11. **The home page is a brand statement; depth lives one click away.** *(Added after Batch 2.)*
   392→   392→12. **3D belongs on one surface, not site-wide.** *(Added after Batch 3.)*
   393→   393→13. **The metaphor shapes labels and metadata, not content shapes.** *(Added after Batch 3.)*
