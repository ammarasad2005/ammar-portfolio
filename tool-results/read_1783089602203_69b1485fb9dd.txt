    82Ôćĺ
    83ÔćĺAfter 20 portfolios, six signals are stable enough to call "codified":
    84Ôćĺ
    85Ôćĺ1. **Commitment beats kitchen-sink.** Cade (1 keyframe) and Whilmar (3 keyframes + WebGL) both feel senior because each commits to a register. Ghulam (86 keyframes) does not. The whole site must sing in one key.
    86Ôćĺ2. **Tinted-neutral background, never pure.** Warm-tinted lights (peach `rgb(254,215,170)`, cream) and tinted-darks (navy `#1E2749`, near-black-warm `#0D0D0E`) both work. Pure black/white is what to avoid ÔÇö *unless* paired with ultralight (200-300 weight) display type at very large sizes (Electric Magic Factory's 109px weight-200 Soehne on pure white). The pure-white escape hatch requires typographic confidence.
    87Ôćĺ3. **Domain-native conceptual metaphor shapes content architecture.** Triet (Unix shell), Whilmar (architectural-engineering), Zyon (Steam achievements) ÔÇö each reframes section labels, hero metadata, and footer copy through the dev's domain. The metaphor shapes content structure, not just decoration.
    88Ôćĺ4. **Two-font developer-coded feel.** Workhorse sans (Inter / Geist Sans / Soehne) + monospace eyebrows on labels (`// about`, `// work`). Universal, low-risk, high-signal.
    89Ôćĺ5. **Hero-only home + linked content subpages.** Nico Bachner (`/projects` + `/notes`), Ajvad (Astro view transitions), EMF (Vue view transitions). The home page is a brand statement; depth lives one click away. Multi-page hybrid with view transitions is the leading architecture.
    90Ôćĺ6. **Footer is a real design surface.** Whilmar's `V2.0.26_STABLE` version stamp, EMF's live local time + timezone + location, Ajvad's live date stamp ÔÇö the footer carries "site is alive" signals that undermine the static-portfolio feeling.

   167Ôćĺ## 2. Pattern Library (Cumulative)
   168Ôćĺ
   169Ôćĺ### Hero-as-Brand-Statement
   170Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Jeremiah Haastrup, Nico Bachner
   171Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: A single saturated color or warm tint + bold typographic statement reads as confident. Removes the pressure to fill a long page.
   172Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: Pair with one secondary "deep dive" surface so the confidence doesn't read as evasiveness. Nico's `/projects` + `/notes` solves this.
   173Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Pairs with two-font system, hero-only home + content subpages.
   174Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: low
   175Ôćĺ- **Performance**: excellent
   176Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: requires care with color contrast on saturated backgrounds.
   177Ôćĺ- **Adopt**: yes ÔÇö as hero philosophy, with linked content subpages for depth.
   178Ôćĺ
   179Ôćĺ### Domain-Native Conceptual Metaphor
   180Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Triet Thai (Unix shell), Whilmar Bitoco (architectural-engineering), Zyon Tiangson (Steam achievements)
   181Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: Reframes section labels, hero metadata, and footer copy through the dev's domain. Signals "I think in this medium" instantly.
   182Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: For full-stack, choose a metaphor broad enough to span frontend/backend/infra/design ÔÇö without becoming a diluted "tech-y" aesthetic. The metaphor should shape *labels* (eyebrows, metadata, version stamps), not force awkward content shapes.
   183Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Strong with mono-eyebrow labels. Conflicts with Brutalism or playful illustration.
   184Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: medium (requires content discipline)
   185Ôćĺ- **Performance**: excellent
   186Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: AT reads metaphor syntax literally ÔÇö need `aria-label` overrides.
   187Ôćĺ- **Adopt**: yes ÔÇö as a *light* metaphor (monospace metadata eyebrows, version stamps, status strings).
   188Ôćĺ
   189Ôćĺ### Two-Font Developer-Coded Feel
   190Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Ant├┤nio Junior, Triet Thai (single-font extreme), Whilmar Bitoco, Nico Bachner, EMF
   191Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: Workhorse sans (Inter/Geist/Soehne) + monospace eyebrows on labels (`// about`, `// work`) reads as "developer" without green-on-black terminal clich├ę.
   192Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: Vary the eyebrow treatment; don't apply the same `// X` formula to every section.
   193Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   194Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: low
   195Ôćĺ- **Performance**: trivial
   196Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: Excellent ÔÇö mono labels are short and screen-reader friendly.
   197Ôćĺ- **Adopt**: yes ÔÇö primary candidate for our design system.
   198Ôćĺ
   199Ôćĺ### Metadata-as-Hero-Content
   200Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Triet Thai (`loc:`, `uptime:`, `status:`), Whilmar (`REF:` / `ARCHITECTURAL SCALE:`), Zyon (Steam-style metadata)
   201Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: Replaces the clich├ę "Hi, I'm X. A passionate Y" with terse machine-readable metadata. Reads as confident, current, and developer-native.
   202Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: Limit to 3-4 fields (location, status, focus, availability). Use `<dl>` for clean AT output.
   203Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Pairs with mono-eyebrow pattern and domain-native metaphor.
   204Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: low
   205Ôćĺ- **Performance**: trivial
   206Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: Use `<dl><dt><dd>` structure.
   207Ôćĺ- **Adopt**: yes.
   208Ôćĺ
   209Ôćĺ### Hero-Only Home + Content Subpages
   210Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Nico Bachner (`/projects` + `/notes`), Ajvad Laseen (Astro view transitions), EMF (Vue view transitions)
   211Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: Home page is a brand statement; depth lives one click away. Combines confidence of hero-only with depth of multi-page.
   212Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: Use View Transitions API with shared-element names for designed page transitions (EMF pattern).
   213Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Pairs with hero-as-brand-statement, view transitions.
   214Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: medium
   215Ôćĺ- **Performance**: excellent (static-rendered subpages)
   216Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: Standard nav semantics.
   217Ôćĺ- **Adopt**: yes ÔÇö primary architecture. *(Note: project constraint ÔÇö only `/` route is user-visible. Will adapt to single-page with section "routes" via scroll/anchor + modal case studies.)*
   218Ôćĺ
   219Ôćĺ### Tinted-Neutral Background
   220Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Nico (peach `rgb(254,215,170)`), Whilmar (near-black-warm `#0D0D0E`), Ajvad (navy `#1E2749`), EMF (pure white with ultralight type)
   221Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: Tinted neutrals feel intentional; pure black/white feel default.
   222Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: Provide both light + dark themes; tint both (warm cream light, warm-black dark). Pure white only with ultralight display type.
   223Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   224Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: low
   225Ôćĺ- **Performance**: trivial
   226Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: Must meet contrast AA in both themes.
   227Ôćĺ- **Adopt**: yes ÔÇö tinted light + tinted dark; user-toggle with system preference default.
   228Ôćĺ
   229Ôćĺ### Numbered Projects
   230Ôćĺ- **Sources**: Phat Tran Tan (`01 02 03 04`)
   231Ôćĺ- **Why it works**: Indexes the work without showing it all at once. Reads as catalog, not gallery.
   232Ôćĺ- **Possible improvements**: Use as nav for a project list view, not just decorative.
   233Ôćĺ- **Compatibility**: Universal.
   234Ôćĺ- **Complexity**: low
   235Ôćĺ- **Performance**: trivial
   236Ôćĺ- **Accessibility**: Provide `aria-label="Project 1: Name"`.

   379Ôćĺ## 5. Provisional Design Principles (Revised Continuously)
   380Ôćĺ
   381Ôćĺ1. **Restraint over abundance.** Premium feels come from what is *removed*, not what is added.
   382Ôćĺ2. **Motion must mean something.** No decorative animations; every transition should orient, reveal, or reward.
   383Ôćĺ3. **Typography is the interface.** Most "premium" portfolios lean on type, not chrome.
   384Ôćĺ4. **One signature conceptual metaphor** ÔÇö not just one signature interaction. The metaphor shapes content structure, not just decoration.
   385Ôćĺ5. **Performance is a feature.** A portfolio that loads fast *feels* premium before any visual registers.
   386Ôćĺ6. **Curate ruthlessly.** Three excellent projects beat thirty decent ones.
   387Ôćĺ7. **The tab title is part of the design.** Never leave it as the framework default.
   388Ôćĺ8. **Commit to a register.** Both minimalism and richness work; what fails is the kitchen-sink middle. Pick a register and commit. *(Added after Batch 3.)*
   389Ôćĺ9. **Tinted neutrals, never pure.** Unless paired with ultralight display type at very large sizes. *(Added after Batch 2, refined after Batch 4.)*
   390Ôćĺ10. **The footer is a design surface, not boilerplate.** Use it for "site is alive" signals. *(Added after Batch 3.)*
   391Ôćĺ11. **The home page is a brand statement; depth lives one click away.** *(Added after Batch 2.)*
   392Ôćĺ12. **3D belongs on one surface, not site-wide.** *(Added after Batch 3.)*
   393Ôćĺ13. **The metaphor shapes labels and metadata, not content shapes.** *(Added after Batch 3.)*
